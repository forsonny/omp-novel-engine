# AI-ism Review Report Template

Use this structure when reviewing fiction.

## Overall verdict

Give a direct, honest summary of the passage.

State whether the issue is:

- low AI-ism
- mild over-polish
- medium over-control
- strong AI-ism risk
- mostly natural with a few tics

Do not claim proof of authorship.

## Ratings

Use these labels:

- AI-ism risk: Low, Low-medium, Medium, Medium-high, High
- Pattern confidence: Low, Medium, High
- Authorship confidence: Low, Low-medium, Medium, Medium-high, High

Pattern confidence means confidence that the pattern is present.

Authorship confidence means confidence that the pattern proves AI authorship. This should usually be lower.

## What works

Name the strengths before the critique.

Possible strengths:

- strong scene engine
- clear stakes
- specific sensory detail
- effective voice
- emotional pressure
- good genre fit
- strong motif control
- compelling character tension
- vivid physical action
- effective dialogue

## Count summary

Required for full reviews.

Before discussing categories, provide a count summary table.

Example:

| Category | Count | Highest severity |
|---|---:|---|
| Hard narrator-distance bans | 0 | None |
| Plain physical prose issues | 7 | Medium |
| Flexible AI-ism risks | 12 | Medium |
| Minor or near-miss findings | 4 | Low |

Do not use this table as a substitute for the occurrence inventory.

## Exhaustive occurrence inventory

Required for full reviews.

List every issue or occurrence found. Do not provide only representative examples.

Use this table:

| # | Category | Severity | Exact quote or location | Why it is flagged | Fix strategy |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|

Rules:

- Quote the exact phrase or sentence when possible.
- Use a local scene label if line numbers are unavailable.
- Do not invent line numbers.
- Choose one primary category per occurrence.
- Use secondary tags only when useful.
- Include low-risk findings in the table or in a minor-findings appendix.
- If no occurrences are found in a category, say "None found."

## Main AI-ism patterns

For each pattern, include:

1. Pattern name
2. Risk level for that pattern
3. Why it creates AI-ism
4. Examples from the user's passage
5. Fix method

Use exact quotes from the manuscript for every occurrence when possible.

## Banned narrator-distance constructions

Include this section even if none are found.

List any hard-ban patterns found in narration:

- "the way" comparison
- type-casting phrase
- negative definition chain
- retrospective framing
- significance announcement
- emotional analysis after observation

For each one, provide:

1. The quoted line or phrase
2. Why it creates distance
3. A direct rewrite strategy
4. Optional sample fix

If none are found, say so briefly.

## Why an AI system might produce this

Explain mechanism, not accusation.

Good phrasing:

- This is a common AI-ism because it creates fast clarity.
- This can happen when a model over-optimizes for thematic coherence.
- This pattern feels machine-shaped because it explains the scene's meaning before the reader can infer it.
- This reads like narrator interpretation rather than character perception.

Avoid:

- This proves AI wrote it.
- This is obviously AI.
- No human would write this.

## How to fix it

Give practical revision strategies.

Examples:

- remove duplicate thematic statements
- replace explanation with behavior
- delay interpretation
- keep one strong symbol instead of five
- merge weaker fragments
- add one contradiction to a too-clean character contrast
- replace narrator comparison with direct observation
- replace future-facing narration with present consequence

## Priority revisions

Give 3 to 5 concrete priorities.

Example:

1. Remove the repeated thesis statements near the scene ending.
2. Replace narrator-distance comparisons with direct physical observation.
3. Let the character's realization remain partial.
4. Add one plain physical detail for every heavily lyrical paragraph.
5. Preserve the core motif but cut duplicate restatements.

## Do-not-change notes

Protect what is working.

Examples:

- Do not remove the central emotional wound.
- Do not flatten the voice.
- Do not cut all lyricism.
- Do not remove the motif entirely.
- Do not make the prose generic.
- Do not turn the character into a purely rational actor.

## Sample rewrite

Only include a sample rewrite when useful.

Rules for sample rewrites:

- keep the author's intent
- keep the tone
- reduce over-explanation
- remove narrator-distance tics
- avoid blandness
- do not rewrite more than necessary

## Final diagnosis

End with a concise conclusion.

Good format:

The issue is not lack of talent. The issue is over-completion. The scene understands itself too loudly. The fix is to keep the pressure and reduce the explanation.

# Plain Physical Prose Section for Reports

When relevant, add this section to the review report.

## Plain physical prose check

Rate this as:

- Clean
- Minor issue
- Moderate issue
- Major issue

List every occurrence found in a full review. If the user asks for a brief review, give representative examples plus a count.

Include these categories when present:

1. Decorative verbs
2. Poetic endings
3. Implausibly beautiful observations
4. Over-explained emotion
5. Redundant explanatory sentences
6. Cushioning around hard facts
7. Prose noticing its own cleverness

For each category include:

- all quoted occurrences from the manuscript
- why it feels AI-ish or over-written
- a plain physical rewrite strategy

End with:

- what to preserve
- what to cut
- what to simplify
