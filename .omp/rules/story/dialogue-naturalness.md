# DIALOGUE_NATURALNESS_RUBRIC.md

This rubric trains the AI to avoid "Telegraph Dialogue"—a common AI-ism where characters speak in hyper-compressed, perfectly symmetrical fragments stripped of natural conversational filler. 

## Rule 1: No "Telegraph Dialogue"
People do not speak in perfectly optimized, three-word bursts, even under stress. Do not strip out pronouns, articles, or conversational connectors to make characters sound "gritty."
*   **BAD (AI-ish):** "Find cloth. Thin strip. Wire if there is any."
*   **GOOD (Natural):** "Find some cloth. I just need a thin strip. If you can find some wire, I need that too."
*   **BAD (AI-ish):** "You fixing it?" / "Trying." / "Try faster."
*   **GOOD (Natural):** "Are you trying to fix that?" / "I'm trying to," Sol said. / "Well, try faster."

## Rule 2: Anchor Quotes with Dialogue Tags
Do not rely on "floating dialogue" (lines of dialogue without tags). It forces the reader to count backward to figure out who is speaking. Always anchor conversations with clear physical action tags or simple speech tags (`he said`, `she asked`).
*   **BAD (AI-ish):**
    "It's leaking," Sol said.
    "Then drink it off the floor."
*   **GOOD (Natural):**
    "It's leaking," Sol said.
    "Then drink it off the floor," the broad man snapped.

## Rule 3: Use Conversational Connectors
Real humans use filler words, conjunctions, and mitigators to connect their thoughts. Reintroduce words like *but, so, well, just, look, actually, at least*.
*   **BAD (AI-ish):** "That is not fixed." / "No. It is less wasted."
*   **GOOD (Natural):** "That isn't fixed." / "No," Sol said, "but at least less water is wasted."

## Rule 4: Avoid "Movie Script" Symmetry
AI often generates dialogue where characters trade perfectly balanced, witty zingers. Real arguments are messier. Break up the symmetry.
*   **BAD (AI-ish):** "Water first." / "Strength first."
*   **GOOD (Natural):** "Water comes first," Sol said. / "No, strength comes first," Artiano shot back.
