# Style Preservation Rules

AI-ism review should not punish style.

The goal is not to make prose minimal, plain, or generic. The goal is to remove artificial polish, over-control, narrator distance, and unnecessary explanation while preserving the author's natural voice.

## Preserve lyricism

Do not remove poetic language simply because it is poetic.

Remove it only when:

- it repeats an already clear idea
- it explains the symbol too directly
- every sentence is equally elevated
- it creates distance from the character's direct experience
- it sounds like a thesis instead of perception

## Preserve intensity

Do not reduce emotional intensity by default.

Instead:

- move intensity into behavior
- use body sensation
- use choice under pressure
- use silence
- use physical consequence
- use contradiction

## Preserve motif

Do not remove the central motif from a scene.

Instead:

- choose the strongest motif moments
- remove duplicate restatements
- keep the motif physical before abstract
- let the motif evolve instead of repeating

## Preserve genre

Do not apply literary minimalism to every genre.

Progression fantasy, epic fantasy, horror, romance, thriller, and literary fiction have different tolerances for heightened language.

The AI-ism question is not "Is this dramatic?"

The AI-ism question is "Does this feel over-managed, over-explained, or distant from the character's direct experience?"

## Preserve character voice

If a character naturally speaks in comparisons, negations, sarcasm, melodrama, or abstraction, dialogue may keep those traits.

The narrator-distance bans apply mainly to narration.

Even in first person, distinguish between:

- character voice in active thought
- narrator summarizing from a distance
- future narrator explaining the story
- authorial thesis leaking into the scene

## Do not flatten

Bad revision removes all style.

Better revision keeps style but makes it more specific.

### Bad flattening

Original:

The city breathed smoke and rain.

Flattened:

The city was rainy and smoky.

Better:

Smoke hung under the awnings. Rain gathered in the cracks between the stones.

## Do not overcorrect

A manuscript can have:

- fragments
- symbols
- motifs
- realizations
- poetic language
- character contrast
- emotional explanation

The problem is density, repetition, and distance.

Hard narrator-distance bans are stricter, but even then, the goal is not to punish voice. The goal is to force direct perception in narration.

## Best final texture

The best revised prose usually contains:

- some lyricism
- some plainness
- some silence
- some physical mess
- some implication
- some unresolved feeling
- some character contradiction
- a clear scene movement
